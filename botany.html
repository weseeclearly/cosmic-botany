Mercury's geocentric path traces a 3-lobed rosette. This 3-fold symmetry appears directly in monocot flowers: lilies, irises, tulips carry petals in multiples of three.

♀ Venus & Dicots

Venus traces a 5-petaled rose over 8 Earth years, with exactly 5 inferior conjunctions spaced 144° apart, forming a pentagram. Dicot flowers carry parts in fives.

The 8:5:13 ratio is pure Fibonacci. 8 Earth years = 5 Venus synodic periods = 13 Venus sidereal orbits. The pentagonal geometry of this orbital resonance appears in rose, apple blossom, buttercup, and geranium flowers.

The Archetypal Plant

In Kranich's morphology, each organ of the plant corresponds to a planetary sphere. The whole plant is a terrestrial image of the solar system.

The root contracts toward the earth-point (Moon). The shoot rises vertically toward the cosmic periphery (Sun). Leaf arrangement encodes inner-planet rhythms (Mercury, Venus). The flower opens outward (Venus, Jupiter). The seed compresses all potential into a dormant point (Saturn).

Bud Path-Curves

Lawrence Edwards measured tree bud profiles as projective path-curves. The shape parameter λ oscillated in synchronization with Moon-planet conjunctions over decades of observation.

Oak buds pulsed with the Moon-Mars cycle (780 d). Beech correlated with Moon-Saturn. The λ parameter controls how pointed or rounded the bud profile is; its rhythmic variation over the season maps directly onto synodic periods.

☿♀ Phyllotaxis

Leaves spiral around the stem at the golden angle: 137.508°. This maximizes light exposure using the same irrational ratio — φ — that governs the inner planets' orbital resonances.

Venus's orbital period fits ~1.625× into Earth's year (φ ≈ 1.618). Successive Fibonacci fractions 2/5, 3/8, 5/13 describe both phyllotactic spirals and synodic conjunction counts. The plant builds these ratios into its body geometry.

⊕ Seasonal Chemistry

Leaf pigment transitions track Earth's orbital position. Chlorophyll dominance breaks down past a critical day-length threshold, revealing carotenes and triggering anthocyanin synthesis.

The chemical shift follows solar declination angle through the year. Green → yellow → red maps a pigment phase rotation synchronized to orbital mechanics, a chemical clock keyed to Earth's position around the Sun.

The sidereal period completes when the target planet returns to 0° — one full orbit relative to the fixed stars.

The synodic period completes when Earth and the target planet realign with the Sun. The closing wedge shows this angular gap shrinking to zero.

Outer planets have shorter synodic periods because Earth catches up quickly. Inner planets have longer synodic periods because the gap closes slowly.
